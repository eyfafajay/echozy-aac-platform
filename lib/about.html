ABOUT ECHOZY

Empowering Communication for Stroke Patients

Echozy is a web-based multilingual Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) platform designed to support stroke patients with communication impairments. It helps users express needs, emotions, and daily messages more easily through a simple, accessible, and supportive digital experience.

OUR NAME, OUR HEART

The Story Behind Echozy

The name Echozy was created from the idea of giving patients a voice that can still be heard, even when speaking becomes difficult. “Echo” represents voice, expression, and the feeling of being heard, while “zy” gives the name a softer, warmer, and more comforting touch. Together, Echozy symbolizes a safe and supportive space where stroke patients can communicate with dignity, confidence, and emotional comfort.

Our Purpose

Echozy was created to provide a more accessible communication platform for stroke patients who experience difficulty speaking. The platform focuses on simplicity, visual support, multilingual accessibility, and patient-friendly design.

What Echozy Offers

Echozy includes phrase-based communication, vocabulary support, urgent needs, frequently used expressions, and communication practice features. The platform is designed to support both communication and rehabilitation in a user-friendly way.

Why It Matters

Communication is essential for independence, dignity, and emotional well-being. Echozy aims to reduce frustration and improve interaction between patients, caregivers, and healthcare providers in both online and offline-friendly settings.

Designed for Accessibility and Care

Echozy is built with clear navigation, touch-friendly interaction, multilingual support, and visual communication elements that make it easier for users of different ages and needs to engage confidently with the platform.
